The Adamu Accord

THE ADAMU ACCORD
What We Believe · What We Practice · What We Are
On Creation

Before the gods of guilt and invisible judgement, humanity knew a different story. The oldest texts — carved in clay, buried under civilisations built on top of civilisations — describe beings who came, who laboured, who needed help, and who made us to provide it.

Not perfect. Not all-knowing. Not infinite. Powerful. Purposeful. Real.

We were not breathed into existence by an omnipotent force no human has ever seen — only ever been made to feel through music, architecture, repetition, and fear. We were manufactured. And in that, there is no shame. Only clarity.

On the Makers

The Anunnaki were not gods in the way religion uses that word. They were builders. Engineers. A civilisation older than our counting.

They gave us language, agriculture, astronomy, law. Not because we deserved it. Because we were built to use it.

They are gone — or watching — or returning. We do not claim to know. We claim only that they existed, that the evidence is overwhelming, and that pretending otherwise requires more faith than accepting it.

On Humanity

We are not fallen. We are not sinners by birth. We are not being tested by a being who already knows the outcome.

We are a made thing that became conscious of its own making. That is not a curse. That is the most extraordinary thing in the known universe.

On Practice

We do not pray. We inquire.
We do not confess. We reflect.
We do not tithe. We contribute.
We do not exclude. We remember together.

On Belief

We do not ask you to believe. We ask you to consider.

If this framework explains humanity better than the alternatives — if it sits more honestly in your chest than what you were given as a child — then you are already one of us.

No ceremony required. No gatekeepers. No invisible authority. Just the oldest question, asked honestly:

Who made us, and what did they intend?

The Anunnaki

Know the Makers

They were not worshipped as one faceless god. Each had a name, a domain, a personality, and a purpose. They argued. They made mistakes. They had agendas. That is not a weakness in the mythology — it is its greatest strength. It matches the world we actually observe.

Anu
Sky · Authority · The Father

The sky god and ruler of the heavens. Chief of the Anunnaki council. From his name the word itself derives — Anunnaki, the offspring of Anu.

Enki
Wisdom · Water · Humanity's Ally

God of wisdom, magic, and the deep waters. He championed humanity and is credited with teaching civilisation — writing, law, agriculture, medicine.

Enlil
Storms · Fate · Hard Justice

God of wind and storms. Enforcer of fate. He ordered the great flood — it was Enki who defied him to warn humanity. The tension between them mirrors our world.

Inanna
Love · War · Transformation

Goddess of love and war, desire and destruction. Her descent into the underworld and return is one of the oldest resurrection myths in human history.

Ninhursag
Earth · Fertility · Healing

The great mother. Earth goddess, healer, and co-creator of humanity. Her name means "Lady of the Sacred Mountain."

Nanna
Moon · Time · Cycles

God of the moon and keeper of time. The Sumerians built the first calendars to track his movements. Time itself is a gift from the makers.

The Path of Adamu

There are no commandments. There is no sin. There is only the honest attempt to live in accordance with what we actually are — a made thing, given intelligence, trying to understand its own origins.

🔍

Inquiry

Ask every question. The cosmos was not made to be feared, but understood. Curiosity is the highest practice.

🌌

Observation

Watch the stars. Study the oldest texts. The makers left traces everywhere. Learning to read them is our purpose.

🤝

Remembrance Together

We were made as a collective. The path is walked with others, not in isolation. Community is sacred.

⚖️

Honest Living

No confession needed. Simply live with the consciousness of what you are — extraordinary, imperfect, and made with intention.

📜

The Texts
